Participant Information Sheet & Consent

What is this document

This document explains what kind of study we're conducting, what your rights are, and what will be done with your data.

Nature of the study

You are invited to participate in an online study investigating how people learn and revise causal reasoning strategies. The study will be completed remotely through an online survey platform and will take approximately 18 minutes to complete. You are asked to use a laptop to complete the study.

During the study, you will view a series of visual displays and causal learning tasks. You will be asked to make judgments, selections, or decisions about causal relationships and may be asked to provide brief explanations for some of your choices. All responses will be submitted electronically through the online survey platform. No face-to-face interaction with the researcher is required. No audio or video recordings will be collected.

We will collect:

  • your responses to the task;
  • response timings;
  • basic demographic information such as age range and gender (optional); and
  • a Prolific/worker ID solely to prevent duplicate participation and process compensation.

We will not collect names, dates of birth, addresses, or other directly identifying personal information unless explicitly stated.

Compensation

You will not be paid for participating in this study.

Risks and benefits

There are no known risks to participation in this study. Other than the payment mentioned, there are no direct tangible benefits; however, you will be contributing to our knowledge about reasoning.

Confidentiality and use of data

All information collected during this study will be processed in accordance with UK Data Protection Law and the University of Edinburgh data protection policies. Participants will be identified using a unique participant number rather than their name. We will temporarily collect Prolific/worker IDs for participant management and prevention of repeat participation. These IDs will be stored separately from study responses and will not appear in research publications or shared datasets. The study data will be anonymised before analysis and publication. Only the research team will have access to identifiable information. All electronic data will be stored on password-protected University-approved storage systems accessible only to the research team. Data will be retained securely in accordance with University policy and then deleted or fully anonymised.

What are my data protection rights

The University of Edinburgh is a Data Controller for the information you provide. You have the right to access information held about you. Your right of access can be exercised in accordance with Data Protection Law. You also have other rights including rights of correction, erasure and objection. For more details, including the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office, please visit www.ico.org.uk. Questions, comments and requests about your personal data can also be sent to the University Data Protection Officer at dpo@ed.ac.uk.

Voluntary participation and right to withdraw

Your participation is voluntary, and you may withdraw from the study at any time and for any reason. If you withdraw from the study during or after data gathering, we will delete your data and there is no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.

If you have any questions about what you've just read, please feel free to ask, or contact us later. You can contact us by email at s2070500@ed.ac.uk. This project has been approved by the School of Informatics Ethics Committee. If you have questions or comments regarding your rights as a participant, they can be contacted at inf-ethics@inf.ed.ac.uk.
